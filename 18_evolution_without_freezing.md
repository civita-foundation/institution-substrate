## Purpose

This file defines how institutions **change over time** without either:
- collapsing under reform pressure, or
- freezing themselves to avoid risk.

It formalizes **evolution as a controlled, evidence-preserving process**, not as political rupture or continuous improvisation.

---

## The Core Problem: Freeze vs Collapse

Institutions face a false binary:

- **Freeze**  
  Preserve stability by resisting change → drift accumulates invisibly.

- **Collapse**  
  Attempt reform via overhaul → legitimacy, trust, and continuity break.

Most institutions oscillate between these two failure modes.

This is not a leadership problem.  
It is an **architectural deficiency**.

---

## Why Evolution Is Hard for Institutions

Institutions fail to evolve because they lack:

1. **Stable Execution Baselines**  
   You cannot change what you cannot reliably measure.

2. **Temporal Comparability**  
   Past and present states cannot be compared without narrative bias.

3. **Reversibility**  
   Change is treated as irreversible, so resistance increases.

4. **Isolation of Change**  
   Experiments contaminate live systems.

As a result, institutions treat evolution as existential risk.

---

## Evolution Is Not Optimization

Institutional evolution is not:
- continuous improvement,
- efficiency tuning,
- best-practice adoption.

Evolution is:

> The controlled alteration of institutional constraints while preserving legitimacy, continuity, and falsifiability.

This requires **memory**, **versioning**, and **containment**.

**Energy Dependency (ARC-E):**  
Institutional evolution requires reconfigurable energy pathways; frozen institutions trap energy and collapse under changing constraints.


---

## DNI Enables Evolution Without Freezing

A Digital-Native Institution enables evolution because it introduces:

### 1. Versioned Rules
Rules are explicit artifacts.
They can be revised without erasing prior versions.

### 2. Parallel Execution
New rules can be tested without overriding live execution.

### 3. Evidence-Preserving Change
TDV ensures that:
- what changed,
- when it changed,
- and why it changed  
remain reconstructible.

### 4. Bounded Rollback
Reversal does not imply guilt or collapse.
It is a valid outcome.

This changes the political economy of reform.

---

## Evolution as a First-Class Institutional Operation

In a DNI, evolution is:

- expected,
- structured,
- auditable,
- survivable.

Evolution becomes:
- technical rather than ideological,
- incremental rather than revolutionary,
- reversible rather than terminal.

This is the opposite of institutional freezing.

---

## What Evolution Does *Not* Mean

Evolution does not mean:
- permanent experimentation,
- governance by A/B testing,
- algorithmic optimization of values.

Evolution is constrained by:
- SCT-F feasibility,
- explicit human judgment boundaries,
- non-delegable moral authority.

Execution evolves.
Values do not auto-update.

---

## Failure Modes of Evolution

Even DNIs can fail to evolve when:

- feasibility constraints are ignored,
- rule complexity exceeds human comprehensibility,
- evolution cadence exceeds trust regeneration,
- environments change faster than institutional learning.

Evolution is bounded.
Honest stagnation is preferable to incoherent motion.

---

## Summary

Institutions fail not because they cannot change,
but because they cannot change **without destroying themselves**.

DNIs introduce a third path:

> **Evolution without freezing**  
> Change without amnesia  
> Adaptation without collapse  

This is not progress ideology.
It is survivability engineering.
